D3LEM — Indian Legal Entity Map

Structural map of the Indian judicial ecosystem. Not case outcomes — how courts, tribunals and regulatory bodies are built, funded, appointed and held accountable.

Scores

Independence Risk (0–15+)

Measures structural vulnerability of an entity to executive influence.

Appointed by executive body+3
Reappointment by same executive+2
Funder = Appointing authority+2
No external complaint mechanism+2
No public appointment criteria+1
Removal by appointer, no committee+1
Constitutional basis−2
Collegium-appointed−2
Parliamentary removal−1

Levels: Low (0–2) · Moderate (3–5) · High (6–8) · Severe (9+)

Structural Health Score (0.0–1.0)

Composite score for policy dashboard. Components:

  • Independence Risk — 30%
  • Case clog severity — 25%
  • Gap severity — 25%
  • Appellate path operational — 10%
  • Vacancy rate — 10%

Not_Constituted / De_Facto_Blocked entities are hard-capped at 0.20.

Health: Critical (<0.3) · Concerning (0.3–0.5) · Moderate (0.5–0.7) · Healthy (0.7–1.0)

Clog Severity

From disposal rate (disposed ÷ filed) and avg disposal days.

  • Critical: rate <0.85 AND days >730
  • High: rate <0.95 OR days >365
  • Moderate: rate <1.0
  • Low: rate ≥1.0 AND days ≤365

Markers

  • * red asterisk — documented structural gap
  • ⟳ — structural circularity (appointer is also litigant, etc.)
  • ⊘ — appellate path blocked in practice
  • NC — legislated but not yet constituted
  • EX amber stripe — structural exception (deviates from statutory template)
  • UT teal — Union Territory (no legislature or limited legislature)
  • Double ring — shared jurisdiction across multiple states
